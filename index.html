Antigone Interactive Lesson Planner

The Antigone Project

An Interactive Backward Design Lesson Planner

Stage 1: Desired Results

Enduring Understandings

  • The concept of justice is complex, often creating conflict between divine law, state law, and individual conscience.
  • Tensions arise when an individual's moral obligations clash with the decrees of a governing power.
  • Excessive pride (Hubris) and inflexibility can lead to tragic outcomes for individuals and communities.
  • While fate is a powerful force, individual choices have cascading and critical consequences.
  • Societal expectations, particularly around gender, significantly influence characters' actions and fates.

Essential Questions

  • When is it justifiable to defy authority?
  • Whose laws hold greater power: the gods', the state's, or one's own conscience?
  • What is the difference between being principled and being stubborn?
  • To what extent do our choices determine our destiny?
  • How do societal expectations shape our identity and actions?

Stage 2: Assessment Evidence

Summative Performance Tasks

The Chorus's Verdict

Create a multimedia presentation from the chorus's perspective, delivering a final judgment on the play's events, referencing at least three essential questions.

A Letter to a Character

Write a persuasive letter to Antigone or Creon at a critical moment, arguing for a different course of action, demonstrating understanding of their motivations.

Modern Adaptation

Create a storyboard or script excerpt for a modern adaptation of *Antigone*, choosing a contemporary conflict that reflects the play's themes.

Stage 3: Learning Plan (The Interactive Experience)

The website itself is the learning plan, guiding students through the content with interactive elements.

  • Character Polls: Embedded polls to gauge student interpretation in real-time.
  • Argument Scales: Interactive tool to visually weigh the competing values in the play's central conflicts.
  • Modern Parallels Forum: A space for students to connect the play's themes to contemporary events.
  • Multimedia Integration: The text is enhanced with audio clips, image galleries, and contextual "Director's Notes."
  • Character Dossiers: Interactive webs exploring character relationships and motivations.

Reading Literature (CC.1.3)

CC.1.3.9-10.A (Themes & Central Ideas)

Alignment: The "Enduring Understandings" and "Essential Questions" are designed to make students trace these core themes throughout the entire play.

CC.1.3.9-10.C (Character Development)

Alignment: "Character Dossiers" and interactive diagnostics require analysis of complex, conflicting motivations (Antigone vs. Creon) and how they drive the plot.

CC.1.3.9-10.G (Comparing Mediums)

Alignment: The "Echoes Through Time" and "World of the Play" sections prompt comparison between the original play and other artistic or modern interpretations of its conflicts.

Writing (CC.1.4)

CC.1.4.9-10.C (Argumentative Writing)

Alignment: The "Letter to a Character" task is a direct exercise in building an argument with textual evidence to support a claim.

CC.1.4.9-10.I (Drawing Evidence)

Alignment: All summative tasks and most formative assessments (e.g., "Argument Scales") require students to constantly return to the text to justify their reasoning and analysis.

Speaking & Listening (CC.1.5)

CC.1.5.9-10.A (Collaborative Discussions)

Alignment: The "Modern Parallels Forum" provides a platform for students to engage with peers, build on ideas, and express their own perspectives clearly.

CC.1.5.9-10.D (Presenting Information)

Alignment: The "Chorus's Verdict" multimedia project requires students to present their findings and analysis logically and clearly for an audience.
